July 15, 2014 - Week 13 is Upon Us!

Week 13 is upon us. Again. As always, it’s a final chance for iRacers from Rookie to Class B to ascend to the next rung on the license ladder in time for Season 3 . . . and time for iRacers of all stripes and licenses to have oodles of fun.

Road and oval racers alike have an opportunity to advance to the next license level in two official series. On the road side there’s the Mazda Cup featuring the Mazda MX-5 Cup with 20 lap races at Okayama (short and long), Charlotte (road), Mazda Laguna Seca Raceway, Summit Point (full course and Jefferson reverse) and Lime Rock. Oval racers can tackle the Legends Cup with 30 lappers in the ’34 Legends Ford at South Boston, Thompson, Oxford Plains, Charlotte (Legends) and Lanier National Speedway.

Safety Ratings and iRatings are in effect in these two official series, offering competitors an opportunity to boost their license ratings before promotions for Season 3 are finalized at week’s end. Likewise, rookies who have yet to complete the category-specific minimum participation requirements for graduation to a D license (two official races or four time trials in each category) can do so in Week 13.

Week 13’s unofficial competition sees the return of the Carburetor Cup at Talladega Superspeedway featuring the Gen6 Chevy SS, Ford Fusion and Toyota Camry.

Want more? How about 144 hours of fun? For the arithmetically-challenged, that breaks down to six (very) different 24 Hours of Fun series.

When the build is released, 24 Hours of Fun I will see the Nationwide Ford Mustang and the Chevrolet Camaro in 50 lap races at the updated Phoenix International Raceway.

Likewise, when the new build is available, 24 Hours of Fun II will pit the GT3 cars (BMW, McLaren and RUF-Track) for 22 lap events on our newest road course, historic Donington Park Racing Circuit.

The 24 Hours of Fun III has our fastest open wheel cars – the Williams-Toyota FW31 and both the IndyCar Dallara and Dallara DW12 – tackling the Daytona road course in 25 lap races.

The 24 Hours of Fun IV brings the Star Mazda to Lucas Oil Raceway for 50 lappers in an online version of the famed Night Before the 500.

24 Hours of Fun V? That would be something completely different, as in the SpecRacer Ford for 15 laps around Irwindale Speedway’s figure 8 layout.

And last but by no means least, 24 Hours of Fun VI boasts 46 car fields of Mazda MX-5 Cups and Roadsters, Pontiac Solstices, ’34 Legend-Fords and SpecRacer Fords at Laguna Seca in 25 lap events guaranteed to put the “mixed” in mixed class sim racing.
